## 6. THE PAIN IS REAL — Evidence

### Over 60% of Startups Struggle with ROC Compliance
> "Over 60% of Indian startups struggle with ROC compliance, often facing hefty penalties that could have been easily avoided."
> — [Jordensky](https://www.jordensky.com/blog/complete-guide-to-roc-filing-services-for-indian-startups)

### 80% of First-Time Filers Hit XBRL Errors
> "80% of first-time filers encounter XBRL validation errors, often requiring multiple resubmissions and deadline extensions."
> — [Jordensky](https://www.jordensky.com/blog/complete-guide-to-roc-filing-services-for-indian-startups)

### Closing a Company Is a Nightmare Too
> "If you fail to follow the right closure procedure, the business legally remains in existence, penalties can escalate, notices of compliance can still be registered, and credit ratings are also affected."
> — [Equimerger](https://www.equimerger.com/blog/close-private-limited-company-india-guide)

- Closing takes **4-6 months** and costs **Rs 12,000-20,000+** (plus penalty cleanup)
- Non-compliance penalties continue at **Rs 100/day** even if business has stopped operations
- Sources: [ClearTax](https://cleartax.in/s/winding-up-private-limited-company), [Jordensky](https://www.jordensky.com/blog/how-to-close-a-private-limited-company-in-india)

### Penalties Compound Quickly
- Late ROC filing: **Rs 100/day per form** for company + **Rs 100/day per director**
- GST late fee: **Rs 50/day per return** (Rs 20/day for nil)
- Director disqualification after **3 years** of non-filing
- GST registration cancelled after **6 months** of non-filing
- Sources: [Ushma Associates](https://ushmaassociates.com/penalties-for-non-compliance-with-roc-filings-in-2025/), [ClearTax](https://cleartax.in/s/gst-registration-suo-moto-cancellation)

### Specific Case Example
> "A Mumbai-based fintech startup that missed their annual filing deadline by just 15 days faced a penalty of Rs 50,000."
> — [Jordensky](https://www.jordensky.com/blog/complete-guide-to-roc-filing-services-for-indian-startups)

## 7. SUMMARY: THE CASE FOR AUTMN

### The Problem (by the numbers)
- **20 lakh** active companies need compliance every year
- **1.13 lakh** new companies registered every year (growing 37% YoY)
- **63+ mandatory filings** per company per year (minimum)
- **1,450+ compliance obligations** for MSMEs annually
- **42 regulatory changes per day** to keep track of
- **Rs 13-17 lakh** spent on compliance per MSME per year
- **Rs 2,000 crore** collected in GST late fees alone, annually
- **9.32 lakh** companies struck off for non-compliance
- **3.09 lakh** directors disqualified and banned for 5 years
- **60%** of startups struggle with ROC compliance

### The Market Opportunity
- India LegalTech market: **$464M (2023) -> $1.25B (2030)** at 15.2% CAGR
- LegalTech funding surged **781% in 2025** ($119M raised)
- Only **1.6 lakh CAs** actively practicing for **20 lakh companies**
- ClearTax valued at **$700M**, IndiaFilings raised **$511M**
- **96%** of companies are Private Limited (AUTMN's target segment)

### Why It's Getting Worse
- GST complexity increasing (IMS, e-invoicing thresholds, sequential filing)
- MCA migrating to V3 with 38 new XML-based forms
- 9,331 regulatory updates in a single year
- More companies registering every year (8.5% CAGR)
- Digital India pushing everything online (but increasing technical complexity)
